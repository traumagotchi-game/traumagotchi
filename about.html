Traumagotchi imagines a future where a person's trauma may be ejected from their mind/body and delegated to live in a pixel-imperfect reality — as their virtual pet.

The Traumagotchi can be fed, bathed, and rested. By accumulating points through playing mini-games, the player can adorn their Traumagotchi with charms or ask it to repeat actions. Though the user may log off, their Traumagotchi persists in cyberspace maintaining preprogrammed cycles of self-care or self-harm. It may recite mantras, tend to altars, do drugs, apply moisturizer, or sink into slime.

They are designed to process your trauma — we all have some.

Conceptually, Traumagotchi explores not only personal trauma, but also the trauma inherent to the rift between our physical and digital identities. An inquiry into the limits of computation, it asks: is it possible to task virtual beings with our psychological processes?
